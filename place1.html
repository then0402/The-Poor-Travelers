Kuching Waterfront, is the most popular gathering place for locals in this capital city. It is located next to the most famous river in the region, Sarawak River, and an esplanade stretches for about 1 kilometer along the south bank of the river. The street is dotted with food stalls, a restaurant, bistro and street food hawkers.Visitors can choose from a variety of foods, whether itâ€™s Malaysian local snacks, such as keropok lekor (fried fish sausage), desserts, such as palm sugar flavoured ice cream, or drinks, such as coconut water.

There is a lot of interesting places should be visited around Kuching Waterfront:


Darul Hana Bridge, as Kuching City's latest landamark, is a "S" shaped bridge spanning the Sarawak River at Kuching Waterfront. The bridge links the north and the south of Kuching over Sarawak River, to reflect the unity and harmonis society in Sarawak.

At night, buskers fill the air with ethnic and contemporary songs and music while the Darul Hana Musical Fountain comes on with its colourful display.There are two shows from Mondays to Thursdays at 8.30pm to 8.45pm and 9.30pm to 9.45pm. whereas three shows from Fridays to Sundays at 8.30pm to 8.45pm, 9.30pm to 9.45pm and 10.30pm to 10.45pm.

Location of Darul Hana Bridge: 93000 Kuching, Sarawak
Distance from airport: 15.9km
Transport suggested: Grab car


Padang Merdeka is an another important place in Sarawak. The field was known as Padang Central until early 1990's. Kuching host the National Day's Parade on it, which is the first having outside of Kuala Lumpur. Due to the influence of Dataran Merdeka in Kuala Lumpur, and the field is smaller and less accomadation surround it compared with Dataran in Kuala Lumpur, therefore the field is named as Padang Merdeka.

The landmark in Padang Merdeka is a silk cotton tree. it is a big tree with an age of almost 2 century, and it is marked and gazette by Sarawak Government as Sarawak national treasure. The tree will produce flower once a year without an exact time and the flowers looked like snow when it fall. People like to gather under the tree and collect the flowers.

Location of Padang Merdeka: Jalan Tun Abang Haji Openg, 93400 Kuching, Sarawak
Distance from airport: 9.8km
Transport suggested: Grab car


Carpenter Street is unofficially regarded as Kuching's Chinatown. it was the centre of Chinese immigrants to Kuching as well as those who came due to Rajah Brooke's promising campaigns.Since it is the cultural heart of the Chinese community, there are many buildings with strong Chinese culture including temples, culture centres, food courts and cafes.

it was initially known as Attap Road because of the use of nipah in the buildings, but the Great Kuching Fire in 1884 burned down more than 160 shops. The buildings were rebuilt with brick. The street's name changed to Carpenter Street to reflect many woodwork workshops in the area.

Location of Carpenter Street: 93000 Kuching, Sarawak
Distance from airport: 9.8km
Transport suggested: Grab car
